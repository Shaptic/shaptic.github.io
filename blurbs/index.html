Blurbs

I hate Twitter, but enjoy the idea of “tweeting”. That’s what this page is about: a pure, short-form stream of consciousness on a site that doesn’t use my thoughts to show me relevant advertisements or radicalize me. Don’t even bother reading these, honestly; almost nothing that comes out of my mouth is worthy of an audience. This is mostly just a way for me to get scattered thoughts out of my head.

  • Suppose you wanted to graffiti someone else’s fence without getting caught. The color of the graffiti isn’t important; it’s about sending a message. That means you can choose any color paint you want. Naturally, everyone in the neighborhood has access to the same colors (so no particular choice would draw suspicion) except for one: it’s a special, rare color that you are known for producing. You aren’t the only one who has any, but you did invent it.

    Now, are you going to use that color to paint the fence?

    That’s how you sound when you say Russia used Novichok to poison people.

  • Has anyone ever considered the fact that since Google is often the provider of free WiFi at most Starbucks, it can secretly man-in-the-middle all of the hotspot’s traffic since it’s also a trusted SSL certificate provider? Of course, this would be relatively easy to catch (for example, by noticing that Bing’s cert is verified by Google rather than Microsoft), but the potential is still unnerving… All it would take is a few targeted, hard-to-spot interceptions to gain backdoors on many, many systems.
