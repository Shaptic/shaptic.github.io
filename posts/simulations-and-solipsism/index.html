Dump of thoughts

first, chain of reasoning that leads to simulation hypothesis: we are getting better and better at simulating things, therefore eventually you could get so good at it that you could simulate our universe.

thus, if simulation exists, a society capable of simulation exists outside of us.

we know from the premise (i.e. the chain to the simulation hypothesis) that there is a scale or spectrum of simulation that is possible and is traversed as the civilization advances.

we know from that, by looking at our own civilization, that the spectrum starts with “single-player” (i.e. local?) simulation and must advance to “multi-player” simulation.

from that, we can create two distinct possibilities if we assume the simulation hypothesis to be true:

  • you are in a single player simulation
  • you are in a multiplayer simulation
  • side note: it’s weird that single player is two words and multiplayer is one

in the first case, you arrive at solipsism: you are the only thing that truly exists in this world. however, it tells you nothing about the external (non-simulated) world. did you program the simulation yourself? that implies there are no external controls, since you wouldn’t be able to both control them and yourself at once. maybe there aren’t any, and this world is as deterministic as you programmed it. or there are other programmers outside of yourself.

in the second case, you now have a new spectrum: there is some number of “players” (at least 2) all the way through to all people being “players”.
